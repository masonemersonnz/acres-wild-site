About the School

Learn to ride
while having fun

Acres Wild Riding School caters for young riders aged 5+, emphasising safety, fun and creating the foundations for aspiring riders of any discipline.

The riding school differs from many in that it's hands on from start to finish, with young riders taught how to catch, groom and feed their ponies right from the get go. This helps them develop confidence and practical skills, essential to having good, safe fun while they learn to ride.

Children are matched to ponies to complement their abilities, whether they are complete novices, or riders aspiring to learn to jump. Owner/Instructor Janine Emerson is now teaching second generation riders in Hawke's Bay, having taught youngsters' mums to ride too.

Lessons available after school Tuesday to Friday, and Saturdays from 9.30am to 12 noon. The ponies rest on Sunday and Monday.

How It Works

What to expect

01

Get in touch

Bookings are essential — lessons fill up fast each school term. Send a message or give Janine a call to check availability and get your child's place sorted.

02

Meet the ponies

From the very first lesson, kids learn to catch, groom and tack up their pony. Every child is matched to a pony that suits their ability — complete beginners to confident jumpers.

03

Ride and grow

Most families book a block of 10 lessons per term to secure their spot. As skills develop, riders progress into jumping and more advanced horsemanship.
